## Blunt Summary
`MidAmerica_V2` is not done. It is more coherent than earlier passes, but it still fails the intended bar in multiple ways:

- It often feels like a very polished concept deck instead of an elite aviation brand site.
- It still relies too heavily on neatly arranged boxes.
- Its dark sections are doing too much structural labor.
- Its mobile rhythm is still weak.
- Several pages are competent, but very few feel unforgettable.

The result is "smart and expensive-adjacent," not "exquisite and decisively premium."

## Highest-Priority Systemic Failures

### 1. Box-On-Box Thinking Still Dominates
The site still solves too many sections with a headline and a set of cards.

Why this fails:
- It makes the design feel system-driven instead of art-directed.
- It reduces luxury to spacing and contrast.
- It creates too much repeated visual grammar across pages.

### 2. Dark Slabs Are Overused As a Rescue Move
The dark sections are often the strongest parts of the pages, but they are being used too repeatedly to create drama.

Why this fails:
- It becomes predictable.
- It can make the page rhythm feel mechanical.
- It suggests the layout needs a dark background to become interesting.

### 3. Heroes Are Still Too Constrained
Most heroes still read as `copy block + image block + side notes`.

Why this fails:
- The pattern is too repeatable.
- It limits surprise and memorability.
- It keeps the site in "premium editorial template" territory.

### 4. Mobile Is Still Not Premium Enough
The mobile pages are clean, but they collapse into long vertical stacks of dark rectangles and pale cards.

Why this fails:
- The rhythm gets monotonous.
- Sections lose individuality.
- The site stops feeling rich and starts feeling procedural.

### 5. Typography Is Better, But Still Not Ruthless Enough
Some headlines still read as tall text masses instead of decisive statements.

Why this fails:
- The site loses confidence.
- Visual hierarchy weakens.
- Wealth signaling depends on confidence, not just nice fonts.

## Page Audit

## Home
Evidence: [audit-index-desktop-full.png](/Users/andrewshea/Desktop/Websites/MidAmerica/review/manual-audit-10/audit-index-desktop-full.png), [audit-home-mobile-full.png](/Users/andrewshea/Desktop/Websites/MidAmerica/review/manual-audit-10/audit-home-mobile-full.png)

### Findings
- The hero is improved, but the lower portion of the image stage still has a dead, pale zone that feels unresolved rather than luxurious.
- The support notes under the hero still read like metadata tiles, not elite trust signals.
- The `Signature Services` slab is stronger than before, but it still feels like a dashboard board dropped onto the page rather than a composition that belongs naturally to the brand.
- The `Operating Standard` strip is too polite and too small to feel like a true proof moment.
- The audience section is structurally clear but emotionally weak; it explains the split instead of dramatizing it.
- The closing CTA is too soft and generic for the final sales moment.

### Standard Failures
- Fails `Every Page Must Have a Strong Composition`
- Fails `White Space Must Feel Intentional, Not Empty`
- Partially fails `Internal Pages Cannot Drop Below the Homepage` because the homepage itself is not yet commanding enough

## Services

### Findings
- The hero rail is still visually secondary and brochure-like even after widening; it feels appended rather than integrated.

- Mobile services remains far too repetitive: dark block, dark block, pale cards, pale cards.

### Standard Failures
- Fails `No Clipping, Cropping, or Awkward Wraps` in earlier service-state screenshots and remains fragile
- Fails `Every Page Must Have a Strong Composition`
- Fails `Internal Pages Cannot Drop Below the Homepage`

## Mobile Audit
Evidence: [audit-home-mobile-full.png](/Users/andrewshea/Desktop/Websites/MidAmerica/review/manual-audit-10/audit-home-mobile-full.png), [audit-services-mobile-full.png](/Users/andrewshea/Desktop/Websites/MidAmerica/review/manual-audit-10/audit-services-mobile-full.png), [audit-contact-mobile-full.png](/Users/andrewshea/Desktop/Websites/MidAmerica/review/manual-audit-10/audit-contact-mobile-full.png)

### Findings
- Mobile is too dependent on stacked dark rectangles.
- Mobile sections are too similar in width, rhythm, and density.
- Too many transitions are `pale section -> dark block -> pale cards -> dark block`.
- The pages are readable but not seductive.
- Mobile still feels like a compressed desktop design, not a separately authored luxury experience.

## Prioritized Punch List

### Priority 1
- Rebuild all internal heroes so they do not use the same `copy + image + note rail` formula.
- Redesign the services page architecture so the major services section is not just a large dark box with smaller boxes inside it.
- Rebuild the About page so it has one dominant, memorable brand-defining composition.
- Redesign contact intake so it feels like a luxury coordination desk, not a styled form shell.

### Priority 2
- Reduce repeated card logic across all proof and manifesto sections.
- Increase compositional asymmetry so each page has a more distinct identity.
- Replace dead pale gaps with either more decisive image treatment or more intentional structure.
- Make mobile rhythm more varied and less block-repetitive.

### Priority 3
- Tighten hero typography further where it still reads too columnar.
- Reduce decorative side-note rails unless they add real functional hierarchy.
- Make final CTA sections more commanding and less polite.

## Bottom Line
The site is improving, but the current state still does not hit the intended clientele bar.

It looks like a thoughtful, premium web project.
It does not yet look like the best aircraft maintenance brand site in the world.

That is still the standard.
